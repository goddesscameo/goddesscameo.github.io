## Intro

Listening to JID’s song “K-Word” off the album “God Does Like Ugly” was what inspired this article. The album revolves around who is worthy of God’s love, an inversion of the American hyperreality which proclaims that God loves the “hardworking” middle class that can do no wrong. The stereotypical image presented in America is almost comical; that of the white picket fence, lack of personal and community problems, ease of accessibility to wants and needs and so forth. Instead, JID claims that no— God loves the downtrodden, he loves the suffering, the lost, the confused. Because if he doesn’t, then who will? The underbelly of America is multi-faceted, however one of the few constants populations that have been neglected are the American Descendants of Slavery. No, Robin DiAngelo’s *White Fragility* has not helped this population, neither did the half-white half-Kenyan president with likely familial CIA connections, nor is the influx of non-American black migrants which move here with an intact family structure, culture, etc. JID essentially says that *God Does Like Ugly*, the inversion of the southern Black American saying “God don’t like ugly”- a judgment on the inevitable justice one will receive through karma, whether through retribution or salvation. The project is a theological inversion of American moral mythology, among many things. No more is this apparent than on the song “K-Word”, with the title clearly being an allusion to karma. Throughout the song, karma is personified as a woman, being responsible for anger, crimes, and temptation (the temptation to blame your situation on karma). Eventually, JID concludes that if he reconciles karma, he can end “the cycle” because he has seen the light. The cycle mentioned here refers to self-reinforcing loops of trauma via gang violence, retaliation, crime, and in general the fatalistic thinking which persists across individuals and generations. The song has a tri-part message it is rejecting: prosperity theology, where success is virtue, karmic determinism where suffering is deserved, and the kind of social nihilism which says that nothing can change. Instead, it proposes that grace exists precisely where the system declares there is none, and that you will get what you deserve in the end. 

In light of this, many people wonder about karma, and more generally, about the law which is responsible for the things in their life. Karma is individual account of a persons’ spiritual and moral history. It is the sum total of everything a soul has done, thought, and felt, which is then transformed and carried forward. Such an account is the raw material, that between lives, is metamorphosed into the forces that shape the next life. A thought or impulse becomes a predisposition for a specific bodily organ or life circumstance. A core definition of karma is the process by which inner, spiritual qualities become outer, physical destiny. This happens in the period between death and a new birth. For example, the sex of a person in one life is the karmic result of the soul’s experiences in the previous life. A woman’s deeply emotional and psychic experiences become the organizing force for a male body in the next incarnation, and vice versa. Similarly, a tendency toward error can, after death, be transformed into a damaged organ in the next life. At the most fundamental level, karma is the process by which the two basic elements of earthly existence - Light (matter) and Love (soul) - are harmonized together, often imperfectly, through the actions of the Luciferic and Ahrimanic beings. Luciferic beings are interested in weaving light into love. When this happens, the soul (love) becomes permeated with a Luciferic element (such as pride or egotism). This impure love then impregnates the physical body (light). This damages the “light” substance of the body, creating a darkness within it. This is what damages the physical manifestation of karma, or what we experience as illness of organic weakness. Therefore, karma is the record of this interweaving. It is the consequence of how the soul has shaped and been shaped by its encounters with the world (light), under the influence of Lucifer and Ahriman. 

Luciferic influence on the karmic process acts from within. This influence temps man through egotism, pride, vanity, ambition, greed, any manifestation of the inflation of the Ego. These are passions that when we succumb, we create a cause that is transformed into a tendency for illness in the next life. Ahrimanic influence acts from without. It is only made possible because Luciferic influence has already created a disorder in the human being, distorting our perception of the external world. Ahriman then insinuates himself into this inaccurate picture, leading to error and materialism. His influence leads to organic damage that may not be accompanied by pain. The two forces are always interacting.

Given this picture, every person is ultimately responsible for themselves, for not falling into sin. It might seem quite individualistic at first, but we will explore this later. Before that exposition, an elaboration on the possibility of justice is necessary. Justice in this process is guided by beneficent powers that counteract Lucifer and Ahriman. When an illness results from a past Luciferic failing, benevolent powers add *pain* to the illness. Pain serves as a subconscious teacher, educating us so we will not repeat the same mistake. Pain can be considered a gift that allows one to escape the clutches of Luciferic temptation. When an Ahrimanic influence damages an organ (precisely by causing the etheric body to penetrate it too deeply, binding us a little too close to the physical world. Lucifer works on the astral, Ahriman on the etheric), benevolent forces step in to destroy or paralyze that organ. This prevents us from becoming hopelessly entangled in Maya, allowing our spirit to find its way back to the spiritual world. The destruction of the organ is always a saving grace. Divine justice on the individual level is an inherent, corrective wisdom implicit in the process and the cosmos, working through these experiences to help guide evolution on the right track. 

### Why do bad people succeed?
This is among the most difficult questions which gnaw at the curiosity of man. Steiner explicitly describes karma as a life-account where good and bad are tallied. The thing to remember is that this account is only balanced over multiple lifetimes, not within one. If a person, such as Bill Gates, is “bad” *and* successful, it means their current life is being lived on the surplus of good deeds from a past life. Their current wrongdoings are creating a new deficit that will have to be balanced in a future life. The apparent success is the interest on an old karmic deposit, and not a reward for current behavior. It is not a blank check either, it might just be one that they can’t cash. Steiner also emphasizes that the consequences of an action do not travel directly from one life to the next as the same thing. They are *transformed* during the long period between death and a new birth. A wrongdoing in one life (such as an act of egotism inspired by Lucifer, or the invocation of black magic) does not cause a punishment. Instead, it is metamorphosed into a tendency, or predisposition, for a specific life circumstance or illness in the next life. This transformation takes time. Therefore, the effect is not immediate in the next life, as if it is a cause-effect relationship. It is already reconciled in the substance of the person’s new body and destiny before they are even born. We see the person's “success”, but we do not see the organic weaknesses or karmic traps they have woven into their own future. 

A point which prevents fatalism is that the persons’ life is not *only* the result of past actions. New, primary actions can enter.

> *"Those things which are due to happen in the course of human evolution must come about through human agency and that these individual agents have to be brought out of the spiritual world, without consideration whether they are fully ready in themselves. They are born for the purposes of human evolution, and a karmic path has to be interrupted or lengthened... In such cases a destiny is thrust upon men which need have no relation to their past karma."*

A “bad” person might be successful because they are an instrument for a larger historical or evolutionary purpose (like Charlemagne [Harun al Rashid who was concurrent with him was better], Aristotle, or Luther). Their personal karmic debt is temporarily set aside so they can fulfill a task for the general karma of humanity. 

Often times, the consequences of wrongdoing are not external misfortunes we can see. They work in the subconscious and in the structure of that persons being. A person who has committed wrong may carry within them a damaged etheric body, or a tendency toward a specific illness that has not yet manifested (or perhaps they are fully depraved and want to *become* the illness such as [Prince Phillip](https://newdailycompass.com/en/the-prince-who-wanted-to-become-a-deadly-virus)). Or they may suffer from a great emptiness. When external life becomes too comfortable (as it does for some “successful” people), the soul experiences a void and rushes from sensation to sensation trying to fill it. This inner hollowness is a form of suffering invisible to an outside observer. 

## Karmic Relationships

![trotskyfreud](https://brilliantmaps.com/wp-content/uploads/vienna_stalin_hitler_trotsky_map.webp)

Some traditions say that incarnation is a result of being caught in a karmic trap, forcing the higher soul to incarnate on the physical world. There is some truth to this. However, Steiner offers a slightly different view, insofar as the conditions under which we incarnate are reconciled with the fact that we do not incarnate in isolation. Souls are bound together through shared experiences across many lives, forming what he calls “karmic groups”. This means that the people in your life, your family, friends, enemies, even brief acquaintances, are likely souls you have traveled with before. The force which draws you together again is the need for karmic adjustment, to balance debts or fulfill connections created in the past. 
> *"What is really guiding this wiser man in us when he wants to meet this or that person? ... we want to meet him because we have met him previously. It may not have happened in the last life; it could have happened much earlier. The wiser man in us leads us to this person because we had dealings with him in a previous life, or because we may have incurred a debt in one way or another. We are led to this person as though by magic."*

There is an aspect, a part of our being, active below the threshold of consciousness, that guides us toward the people we need to meet. This “wiser man” works with the forces inscribed in our astral body during the Kamaloca period after death. When we wrong someone, we *must* experience their pain in that state, and this experience imprints a force that later draws us back to them for resolution. Steiner gives us a remarkable set of rules for *when* in our lives we are most likely to meet people from past incarnations. These are based on the “climax and pivoting point” around our thirties. The first is from birth to ~30 years. We meet with people whom we were connected during the middle period of a previous life. This is why parents, siblings, and childhood playmates are often souls we knew intimately in a past life, at a different stage. The second period is in the “middle” of life, from ~30 to ~50 years of age. We meet with whom we were connected during the early childhood of a previous life. This is also the time when we may encounter those who were our parents in a past life. The second half of life are more complex, it does not follow a static pattern. Meetings may happen with people we were connected with before, but often the karma is too extensive to be resolved in one life. These meetings can begin a process that will span future incarnations.

### Recognizing Karmic Connection
Steiner distinguishes between two different kinds of encounters, which you can identify by the nature of the impression made. The first type is based on the will and the heart. It’s a very deep, immediate pull. You feel that you *must* follow or oppose the person, regardless of their external appearance or intellect. It may manifest as love, hate, or an intense sense of being drawn to people. When discussing “soul ties” and “soul partners” (a topic we will not have time to go over), often times, only a *positive* affinity is discussed, but the possibility of a negative affinity, that two people, maybe who love each other, drive each other to destruction is never considered. The karmic significance of this first type indicates a strong karmic connection from a past life. The will is working before the meeting even takes places, guided by the lunar forces of necessity. It’s not too crazy to think of this in romantic terms either— that the significant other you were immediately drawn to was fated to be met; that all your life lead up to the moment you have met them. However romantic this is, this notion does discount the freedom that we have. 

The second type of karmic connection is based on the intellect and the aesthetic. Admittedly, this is a more superficial judgment. You may admire the person, find them interesting, a quite “splendid fellow”. But they do not affect your deepest will or inner being. All this indicates is that there is no *strong* past karmic bond. This is a new meeting, brought about by solar forces, which can become the basis for future karma. 

> *"If the effect made by the two upon each other reaches into the will, into the heart, into the inmost nature, then a karmic connection exists... If an effect made by another person reaches only into the intellect, into the aesthetic sense, this is not an outcome of the Moon's activity, but a situation brought about by the Sun and one that will have its sequel only in the future."*

### Karmic ties of non-individuals: Humanity and Cultures

I previously mentioned the “general karma of humanity”. What is meant here is that there is an overarching plan of human evolution. Great historical events, like the Persian Wars, cannot be explained by the individual karma of the participants alone. They serve a purpose for the whole of humanity, a continuous stream of civilization guided by normally progressing spiritual hierarchies. A concept known as “impulse groups” is also important here. This concept specifies specific collections of individualities who incarnate together in a particular time and place to generate a major cultural or spiritual impulse. An example can be considered the Florentine Renaissance Group from the 15th century wherein a network of souls around the Medici family, including Ficino, Pico della Mirandola, Botticelli, da Vinci, Michaelangelo, and Raphael incarnated together to foster a rebirth of Platonic and artistic impulses in Europe. The German Idealist group including Goethe, Schiller, Novalis, Hegel, Fichte, and Schelling is another example. Such groups show that entire cultural epochs are the karmic project of specific soul communities, working together over centuries to advance human consciousness. 

Collective/group karma may also express itself through the life of a folk soul or national spirit. An individual incarnates within a particular folk soul for a single lifetime, but the folk soul itself is a distinct higher spiritual being with its own evolution. 

> *"To identify oneself with a folk soul is, in reality, totally devoid of meaning... We shall only understand these things properly, once we can look earnestly and deeply at the truths of reincarnation and karma."*

Additionally, the specific bloodlines and family lineages into which we are born are actively prepared by us in the spiritual world between deaths. For centuries before our birth, we are immersed in the forces of a particular family stream, working to bring about the exact physical and social circumstances we need. 

Having now the distinction between individual and group karma (and furthermore, that of the whole karma of humanity), we can understand aberrations in the “divine plan”. Successive civilizations arise and flourish, each driven by a necessary “illusion” that its own culture is eternal. This enthusiasm, which allows the culture to make its unique contribution to humanity, is the work of Luciferic spirits, who enkindle this fervor into human astral bodies. A civilization’s decline and its treasures being passed on is the work of Ahriman, who “spoils” what Lucifer has created. From this perspective, a social system like the caste system could be understood as a concretization of the perceived spiritual laws of karma and reincarnation, within the “general karma” of a particular cultural epoch. It would be the form in which that culture’s understanding of cosmic justice was lived out, driven by the necessary “illusion” and enthusiasm of its time. The justified position of a Dalit class in India, in this view, would be a misunderstanding, or a hardened, materialist (Ahrimanic) interpretation of a truth about karm/reincarnation, which originally was meant to explain individual growth and freedom, not to eternally fix a social hierarchy. Steiner continuously emphasizes that karma is not “fate” and that we cannot simply say someone’s current misfortune is the karmic result of a past life without knowing the full context, which includes forward-looking purposes as well. 

### Animal Karma
Animals do not have individual karma, in the same way that humans do. Their experiences of life, death, and destiny are governed by a different principle. The main distinction lies in the nature of the soul. Man possesses an individual Ego that reincarnates and carries its karmic account from life to life. Animals, on the other hand, are part of a group soul or generic soul. The group soul is a higher, collective spiritual being that oversees a whole species of group of animals. The individual animal is like a temporary expression or limb of this group soul. Steiner provides an analogy to explain how the group soul experiences the death of an individual animal. It is not a moment of individual destiny, as it is for a human. 

> *"For the group soul the death of an animal resembles somewhat our experience at the approach of summer, when we have our hair cut shorter, which will then slowly grow again. The group soul of a species feels the death of an animal like the death of a limb which will gradually be replaced."*

Because the individual animal is not the permanent bearer of an Ego, it cannot generate its own karma or carry consequences from one physical life to the next. The karmic responsibility and evolution occur at the level of the group soul. The group soul is the entity that evolves, learns, and experiences the consequences of the collective actions of its members across time. The individual animal may be considered a fleeting manifestation of that collective being. 

Although the external events of birth and death look similar for humans and animals, the inner experience is completely different. For a human, birth is a descent into individual consciousness, and death is a gateway to the spiritual world, where the Ego reviews its past life and prepares for the next. For an animal, birth and death are simply the emergence and dissolution of a temporary physical form for the enduring group soul.

## Destiny, The Question of Freedom
Suffering can be a trial, or even a mystery unrelated to personal wrongdoing. There are things beyond individual karma, this is what the Book of Job is partly about. Not everything about a person can be traced back to their own past deeds, this is a contingency of life. Steiner himself makes this point below:


> *"Take the case of Luther. We cannot just simply ascribe everything he experienced and endured to his karma; we must be clear that those things which are due to happen in the course of human evolution must come about through human agency and that these individual agents have to be brought out of the spiritual world, without consideration whether they are fully ready in themselves. They are born for the purposes of human evolution, and a karmic path has to be interrupted or lengthened, so that the individuality concerned may appear at a certain time. In such cases a destiny is thrust upon men which need have no relation to their past karma. "*

Job finds a direct parallel. His suffering is not presented as a punishment for his sins, it is a “destiny thrust upon him”, because his soul was chosen to play a role in a test of faithfulness that serves a purpose beyond his individual karmic account. Steiner gives another concrete example that echoes this principle:

> *"Let us take a case which actually happened. In a natural catastrophe a number of people perished. It is not at all necessary to believe that it was in their karma that they all should thus perish together; this would be a cheap supposition. ... There is an instance that has been investigated of a number of people perishing in an elemental catastrophe which resulted in a close alliance of these people at a later period, and, owing to their common fate, they gained the strength to undertake something in common. Through this catastrophe they were able to turn from materialism and brought with them in their next incarnation a disposition to spirituality."*

In the example above, the catastrophe was certainly not a byproduct of wrongdoing brought by karma. It was an event that came from the general karma of humanity to create a new impulse for the future. 

While events can be beyond an individual person’s karma, these events are not placed in the realm of pure chance, or some inscrutable divine will separate from the law of karma. Steiner expands upon the concept of karma to include the actions and destinies of the gods. The entire 11th lecture of "Manifestations of Karma" (GA 120) is dedicated to this notion. The suffering and struggles of humanity are interwoven from the disappointments and sacrifices of higher beings. From this perspective, an event like Job’s trial is not “beyond karma” in the sense of being a lawless plaything of some God or another. It is part of a larger karmic system involving spiritual beings. Job’s suffering serves a purpose in a dialogue between divine and adversarial powers, karmic interactions at a level far above the human. Steiner even describes the great disappointment of the Luciferic spirits as they see their work constantly destroyed by Ahriman. This is *their* karma. Man, by consciously developing love and wisdom, will one day be able to redeem these higher beings, repaying the karmic debt for the gifts of freedom they bestowed. 

The question still needs to be explored: *how* is karma not fate? How is it not deterministic? Under what circumstances can one say that freedom is an actuality, an actual achievement and not an illusion? The answer is deceivingly simple. Freedom is the goal of the entire karmic process, made possible by the tension between necessity and conscious human development. If karma were fate, your current life would be nothing but the inescapable result of the past. But because *new* causes are constantly being created in the present, the future remains open. The past provides the conditions and tendencies, but it does not dictate every choice. The buck eventually *has* to stop at you and your independence. 

Paradoxically, the possibility of human freedom is directly attributed to Luciferic influence. Without it, humans would have evolved as passive recipients of divine gifts, unable to make independent choices. Lucifer has provided us with the gift of discrimination. The Luciferic beings, by tempting humans with the full range of passions, gave humanity the gift of choice. They introduced the very possibility of error, but also the possibility by consciously choosing the good. This capacity for oscillation between good and evil is the very ground in which a free, self-aware “I” can develop. 

Previously, in the section on “recognizing karmic connection”, mention was made of lunar and solar forces. These are symbolic representations of how necessity and freedom interweave in our lives. Lunar forces govern everything that leads up to a significant event, such as a meeting between two people. Before they meet, their paths are guided by an “iron necessity” stemming from past karma. This is the work of the Moon beings, who have inscribed past experiences into the astral body, creating an unconscious drive that leads two people to the same point in time and space.

> *"what has been in operation between two human beings before they actually meet in earthly life is determined by the Moon... what occurs between two human beings before they become acquainted can only be regarded as the outcome of iron necessity..."*

Sun/solar forces govern everything that happens after the meeting. Once the individuals are face to face, the past has fulfilled its purpose. The present moment, with its conscious choices and impressions, now becomes the scaffolding by which freedom can build or destroy itself. This is the work of the Sun beings. 

> *"...everything that takes place between them after their meeting is determined by the Sun... as the expression of freedom, of mutually free relationship and behaviour."*

The meeting point itself is where the thread of past karma is united with the thread of future karma. What we *do* with the encounter, how we act, what we choose, that creates the new causes that will shape our future destiny. 

If freedom is the goal, how then do we achieve it? Well, that’s the question, isn’t it…

That is a question I refuse to answer, no matter how many times it is brought up. Self-awareness is the only prerequisite. In the future, human evolution of consciousness will have a setting where freedom will be enhanced because we will be able to see karma directly, supposedly. This capacity may even begin to emerge before the middle of the 20th century, according to Steiner. When a person can see, after an action, a picture of what they still owe to balance the scales, their choices become far more conscious and therefore far more free. Acting with blind impulse no longer becomes an option, a clear vision of consequences is available. 

On a final note, it is worth realizing that human freedom is not just for *our* own benefit. Our conscious development of love and wisdom is destined to redeem the very beings who made our freedom possible (Lucifer and Ahriman). Valentin Tomberg has once formulated that the two thieves on the cross represent Lucifer and Ahriman- the one who asks Christ to be remembered, receiving a promise of paradise, was Lucifer. The one who mocked Christ was Ahriman. Thieves indeed, for they have stolen a piece of divinity as Prometheus did fire, Lucifer who enkindled our passions and gave us the capacity for free choice, suffers continual disappointment as his work is destroyed by Ahriman. In the future, humanity will consciously and alchemically transmute Lucifer’s gift of passionate desire into selfless love. We will transform Ahriman’s realm of domain and error and illusion into wisdom. By doing so, we will repay our karmic debt to these higher beings and help them evolve.
