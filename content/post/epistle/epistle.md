## Intro 
Human biodiversity confronts us with a problem. Among the myriad physical features of man such as skin color, muscle and fat distribution and composition, epicanthic folds, head and body shapes, hair, eye color, and even *blood* characteristics, it pushes one towards the verdict that we are in some way *different*. Yet, breeding within two “disparate” populations, producing fully fertile offspring is possible and we, more or less, are capable of communicating, interacting, and understanding the life of each other despite this unnamed difference. The problem we are confronted with is the imposition of difference, with the perceived ontological grounding of oneness. How can we be both *one* (a single species)  and *many* (so visibly diverse)? An answer one often finds aligned with a paradoxically regressive strain of thinking is the conclusion of *polygenism*. Paradoxical since it seems to find a home among those who would ally with the bias of immediacy - such as those who call themselves Christians, or enlightenment thinkers that tend to biological reductionism. The answer they provide is accepting of the immediacy of appearance, as well as behavior, and reduces it to some immutable fact of biology, or in some cases denigrates the status of the spiritual by *raising* appearance and behavior to some discrete and exclusive spiritual condition. Either path is the aforementioned attitude in new skin, mentioned because of the elephant in the room: racism. More will be said on this later as it relates to racial distinctions. For now, only a footnote can be made on the racist, and often Faustian, motivations behind rejecting Out of Africa, as well as upholding the fixed character of “racial” distinctions.

One historical answer to the question of the one and many in man was *polygenism*, the view that different races have separate origins, whether created separately by God or evolved from different apes/hominids. The idea is quite old. For example, the Roman Emperor Julian argued that the Germani and Scythians were too different from the Ethiopians to share common ancestry. Oral traditions such as the Bambuti creation myth told of three races made from three different kinds of Clay. Biblical polygenism regularly draws on the three sons of Noah as stand in for the “races”, or stock, of Black, White, and Yellow. In the 19th century, polygenism became a scientific movement, drawing Enlightenment rationalism and empiricism into it’s supposed methodology. People like Samuel George Morton and Josiah Clark Nott claimed that cranial measurements proved races were separate species. Polygenism persisted, persisted even after Darwin’s Origin of Species, with Carl Vogt and others arguing that different races descended from different apes. These days, modern science has decisively rejected polygenism. The Out of Africa genetic evidence shows that all living humans trace to a common African population within the last 200-300 thousand years. There is no genetic or fossil support for separate origins. Polygenism seems to have failed because it mistook the superficial adaptations for a more prior difference. 

As it appears, the rejection of polygenism does not dissolve the phenomenon of diversity. We are one species, one blood (especially after the time of Jesus), one *recent* origin. And yet we appear in such a variety. My own explorations of this topic takes the unity and diversity seriously as the starting point of a spiritual investigation. Polygenism as an Enlightenment project and mere stipulation was an enterprise dead on arrival, for mainly the same reasons that taxonomy fails at being a science. Taxonomy, which as much as it can list difference in painstaking detail, cannot arrive at the generative process by which the differences in the elements it categorizes come about. Likewise, it cannot supply us with what stands as a valid separation or not. Yet, it was not wrong to sense that the diversity we see has deep roots. What polygenism lacked was a vision of *how* unity could give rise to multiplicity without fracturing into separate species forever. This is where spiritual science may offer us a missing link in the fact that the diversity we observe is a result of a gradual hardening and differentiation from an original spiritual unity. The physical form we now call *Homo sapiens* is the ultimate consolidation of a long process that began when human souls first descended into Earthly bodies in the Lemurian epoch, passed through the more plastic, liquid forms of the Atlantean epoch, and only fully solidified in the Post-Atlantean - the very period that Out of Africa genetic studies takes as it’s given object. 

Why has Out of Africa become the consensus view of modern anthropology? The reason is that the evidence for a recent African origin of Homo sapiens is now so extensive that alternative models, while interesting, have now been set as aside. This is the conclusion that I have ultimately come to. There are a few alternative theories to consider before returning to Out of Africa. The first is the Out of Australia theory. This hypothesis suggested that anatomically modern humans first emerged in Australia, based on the claims of very early archaeological sites like Lake Mungo, as well as the suggestion that *some* ancient Australian remains showed unique morphological features. Proponents of this theory argued that the long temporal depth of human occupation indicates a possible origin. The problem is that all living humans, including Indigenous Australians, carry the same genetic markers that trace back to Africa. The oldest Y-chromosome and mitochondrial DNA lineages are found in *Africa*, not Australia. Moreover, the oldest fossils of Homo sapiens are African, far predating any Australian remains. Early Australian sites indicate an early migration of modern humans *out* of Africa, as opposed to an independent origin. Hominin Network Theory, in recent years, proposes that the fact that Neanderthals and Denisovans interbred with modern humans, creating essentially a tangled web of interbreeding across Eurasia, suggests to us that modern humans did not have a single African origin, but rather emerged from a network of archaic populations across the Old World. This interpretation misreads evidence. Interbreeding *did* occur, although it was limited and one-sided. The Neanderthal and Denisovan DNA found in non-African populations amounts to only 1-6% of their genomes. The remainder is overwhelmingly derived from a single source population. Additionally, no trace of these archaic hominins exists in sub-Saharan African populations, who retain the oldest human lineages. The network model confirms that the African lineage was the dominant one, absorbing small contributions from archaic populations encountered during expansion. The multi-regional model proposed that after *Homo erectus* left Africa nearly 2 million years ago, populations in Africa, Europe, and Asia continued to evolve in parallel, with enough gene flow between them to keep them a single species. Modern humans, in this view, would have emerged simultaneously across the Old World from local archaic ancestors. For decades, this was the main competitor to Out of Africa. There was supporting evidence by some fossil evidence of regional anatomical continuity (such as the fact that certain facial features in East Asia seemed to persist from *Homo erectus* to modern populations). However, when ancient DNA became available, the multiregional model collapsed. Genetic data showed that all non-African populations carry a distinct genetic signature that emerged in Africa within the last 100,000 years and spread outward. There’s no continuity of lineages from pre-sapiens populations in Europe or Asia into modern humans. Only the small admixture noted prior. The scientific case for Out of Africa seems to be settled. But for those who ask *why* humans are so diverse, why *this* particular species expanded so rapidly, and *what* the meaning of our unity and diversity might be, science offers limited answers. The anthroposophic spiritual-scientific framework helps us to situate Out of Africa within a broader narrative, becoming material testament to spiritual reality. 

### Humans and Apes

Before continuing, it is important to note one thing. The animal kingdom (including the apes) did not arise through a linear evolution from lower to higher culminating in humans. Instead, animals represent forms *separated* from the human stream at various stages of Earth evolution. During the Lemurian and Atlantean Epochs, the human physical form remained relatively “plastic” and capable of being shaped by the will, while certain group souls took hold of physical bodies that had hardened prematurely, giving rise to the various animal species. Apes in particular are described as a branch that split off during the Lemurian epoch, when the human form was still soft. Their physical bodies became fixed earlier, while the human form retained its malleability, allowing it to later receive the fully incarnated human “I”. Thus, the anatomical similarities between apes and humans are not evidence of common descent in the Darwinian sense, it is a reflection of a shared origin in the plastic human form from which apes diverged. This has implications for how we interpret the fossil hominins such as australopithecines, *Homo habilis*, *Homo erectus*, and so on. They represent the various side branches or transitional forms that either preceded the full descent of the human “I” into physical bodies, or were left behind as the human form continued to refine and harden. The fact that the first stone tools appear with australopithecines shows that even before the “I” fully incarnated, beings with a degree of intelligence and will were interacting with the physical world. Steiner would likely say that these beings were ensouled by group souls (or by individual souls not yet fully independent) who were preparing the physical “vehicle” for the eventual incarnation of the fully self-conscious “I”. When we come to *Homo sapiens* in the Quaternary period, we see the completion of the process. The physical body is fully hardened, and the human “I” can incarnate into it with full self-consciousness/self-awareness. The Out of Africa populations can be said to represent the stock in which this final hardening and spiritual integration took place.

## Physical Consolidation
The three periods we shall concern ourselves with are the Lemurian, Atlantean, and our current post-Atlantean epochs. The Middle Lemurian period, correlating roughly to the Mesozoic period, was the beginning of physical incarnation. The human body was still soft and malleable, bones were not fully mineralized, and so durable fossils did not exist. This was also following the time the sexed distinction made its emergence. The Atlantean period, correlating roughly to the Tertiary (the Paleogene and Neogene), found a progressive *hardening* of the world and of man. Bodies were much more liquid-like compared to today, yet the skeletal system was still not fully solid. The first durable artifacts of man appear only at the very end. It is with the Post-Atlantean period, correlating to the quaternary (Pleistoscene and Holocene), that “hardening” completed. Bones fully mineralized, the modern brain structure emerged (as evidenced by the [Kalambo structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalambo_structure), demonstrating that even 500,000 years ago, beings on Earth were capable of planning and complex functions), and the human form as we know it today cemented itself. If Steiner’s account is accurate, we should expect the physical record to roughly correlate and show no durable evidence from the Lemurian epoch, because the body, and its lifestyle, did not leave much to be found. We should also find only sporadic, late evidence from the Atlantean epoch, as hardening gradually progressed (we will not touch the topic of inherited Atlantean wisdom in this article). Additionally, we should also find abundant evidence of fully modern humans from the post-Atlantean epoch onward. This is precisely what the fossil and archeological records reveal. During the Lemurian period, there are no accepted hominin fossils or stone tools. The few anomalous claims (such as the Eocene tools of the 19th century) have been refuted as either misinterpretations of the data or frauds. However, this does not serve as a problem, because it is exactly what one would expect if the human form has not yet reached a level of solidity capable of leaving durable remains (and based off the lifestyle of man during the Lemurian, they were much more nature attuned than we are today. The transformation/destruction of nature into tools would not occur until the Atlantean). For most of the period, there is no evidence of “man”. The first hominins appear only in the late Miocene and early Pliocene (*Sahelanthropus, Orrorin, Ardipithecus*), but they left no stone tools. Only at the very end of the Pliocene, around 3.3 million years ago, do we find the first undisputed stone tools at Lomekwi, Kenya. This is the point when Steiner would say hardening was approaching completion. As the Atlantean epoch gives way to the Quaternary, the first durable evidence of a being capable of thought and technology begins to appear. Finally, in the Quaternary period, with the beginning of the Pleistocene after the global flood, the picture begins to resemble what we are familiar with. We find the first fossils of *Homo*, the spread of stone tool industries across Africa and Eurasia, and eventually, around 300 thousand years ago, the appearance of the anatomically modern *Homo sapiens*. It is no coincidence that cognitive and technological sophistication became possible once the physical form had fully hardened. This, along with a myriad of other factors, such as the separation intwo sexes, made it possible for the kind of independent, discriminatory inner life we are familiar with today, including its offspring: technology. 

The Out of Africa genetic evidence places the origin of all living *Homo sapiens* within the Post-Atlantean epoch. The mitochondrial and Y-chromosomal lineages that coalesce around 200-300 thousand years ago represent the survival of a particular population in Africa whose physical constitution had reached the modern level of mineralization. From this population all living humans have a connection. This does not mean that there were no humans before that time. It means that before the Post-Atlantean epoch, the human physical form had not yet achieved the degree of solidity that would allow it to fossilize in the kind of way we recognize today, and that the genetic lineages of those earlier forms either died out or were absorbed into the later, more hardened populations. In this way, the Out of Africa bottleneck is a genetic signature, representing the final stabilization of the modern human body. 

## Origin of the Races
If the human physical form only fully hardened in the Quaternary, then the diversity we see among human populations today *must* have arisen after that consolidation. For those that have followed thus far, this conclusion certainly makes sense. It is also consistent with the scientific view that the major physical differences among human groups are recent adaptations to different climates and environments, occurring within the last 100 thousand years or so. Steiner provides us with another, higher, aspect. The diversity we see appears to us as a matter of natural selection, or this or that theory, acting on a uniform “stock”. It may do something to that effect, but it primarily reflects the lingering influence of the spiritual forces that shaped the human body during the long epochs of hardening. The different “racial” characteristics are expressions of the different planetary and Luciferic influences that worked on the human form during the Lemurian and Atlantean epochs, only becoming fixed in the Post-Atlantean period. 

In the middle of the Lemurian epoch, when the human physical form became capable of hosting the self-conscious “I”, souls began to incarnate from different planetary spheres (the beginning of physical existence, and the sexed distinction, was also the beginning of reincarnation!). This includes beings not only from Earth’s sphere, but also from Saturn, Jupiter, Mars, and other regions. These souls brought with them different spiritual-soul qualities, inclinations, temperaments, and karmic backgrounds that suited them for particular forms of earthly experience. The souls that descended from Saturn, for instance, had a natural affinity for a more inward, contemplative life; those that came from Mars brought a more active, will-oriented nature; those from Jupiter carried a certain wisdom and sense of the cosmic order. At that time, the human physical body was relatively malleable. The group souls that guided these incoming spirits worked from within to shape the body in accordance with the spiritual predisposition of each group. This is why, already in the Lemurian epoch, distinct “types” of humanity began to emerge. They would be tendencies that would later solidify into “races”. 

An important factor in the formation of the races was the influence of the Luciferic beings. Luciferic spirits (particularly the aberrant Spirits of Form, also called the Luciferic Spirits of Motion) intervened in the evolution of humanity during the Lemurian and Atlantean epochs. Their goal was to accelerate the independence of the human “I” at the cost of disrupting the original unity of the human physical and etheric constitution. Had these beings not intervened, humanity would have developed from a single, uniform physical type that would have changed only slowly and in harmony with the Earth’s transformation. Instead, the Luciferic forces introduced *differentiation*. They hardened certain groups more quickly, fixed certain physical traits, and caused the stock of man to split into varied forms that adapted to different geographic and spiritual conditions. Steiner describes the abnormal Spirits of Form, working from the astral, taking hold of the etheric and physical bodies of different groups, imprinting them with characteristics that would later become hereditary. It’s important to clarify that these beings are not evil in the moral sense, but that their activity created the conditions for human diversity. While these also brought along with it the potential for egoism and separation, it also brought the need for the unifying force of the Christ impulse. 

By the Atlantean epoch, the differentiation had crystallized into seven sub-races, from which the main racial types of the current Post-Atlantean epoch descended. At the end of Atlantis, when the continent was destroyed by the great flood (associated with the ice ages at the boundary between the Tertiary and Quaternary periods), populations migrated in different directions. According to Steiner, five main racial remnants emerged from this dispersal. I must mention importantly that these races are not to be confused with the modern day racial categories.

The first is the Black race, These are descendants of the earliest Atlantean sub-races, carrying the oldest physical forms. Their constitution is characterized by a strong astral body, making them receptive to spiritual forces in a more instinctive, dreamlike way. The next is the Yellow race, originating from the later Atlantean sub-races that migrated eastward into Asia. Their etheric body is particularly strong, giving them a higher rapport with the vital forces of nature, expressed in their languages and cultural forms. The next is the Red “American Indian” race, a branch that migrated westward into the Americas. They retained certain archaic qualities, including what Steiner calls a “memory-based consciousness”, and a close relation to the land. This red race of ancient times are not to be confused with the Native Americans of today, they represent a “declining” branch of humanity. More to be said on this later. The penultimate race is the Brown race, a mixed group which spread across the islands of Southeast Asia and the Pacific. Their physical form shows a blend of influences, and their constitution tends towards a certain dreaminess and passivity. Finally is the White race, descended from the fifth Atlantean sub-race (known as the “primal Semites”), which had its main center in what is now Ireland. This group migrated eastward across Europe and Asia, becoming the bearers of logical consciousness, the faculty that would come to full development in the Post-Atlantean epoch. Such migrations established the broad racial categories that have persisted into historical times. However, it is important to understand that the “races” we observe today are not identical to the original Atlantean forms. Over millennia, the intermixing with Homo sapiens and various other hominids, adaptation to new environments, and the ongoing process of soul incarnation have continuously reshaped these populations. The categories and characteristics that seem to hold so much weight today are in fact an outer residue of a process which began in the Lemurian period and is now gradually coming to the end.

As regarding the modern “races”, these five are definitely not “pure” types; they have intermingled throughout history well into our current Post-Atlantean period. The boundaries are fluid, and the characteristics attributed to the archaic races are general tendencies, not static absolutes. Migration and population is a very complex thing, involving moving over time, backflows of migration (creating a recursive situation), and internal differentiation within a given population. Throughout time, a tendency towards the flourishing of the individual has emerged, helped by the fact that Steiner emphasizes that these racial differences are not permanent. In the future, as humanity evolves toward spiritual unity, the concept of race will lose its meaning. ## Adam and Eve Revisited: One and Too Many

What is left after the initially unified “man” having been a purely spiritual form, then concretized and materialized into the many *kinds* of man, is revealed to be a transitionary stage towards a reunion of unity? We may bring to the fore the archetypes which underlie the unity of man: Adam and Eve. It is quite fitting that modern science has taken the symbolic archetypes found in the bible in its monikers of “Y-chromosomal Adam” and “Mitochondrial Eve”. The familiar biblical account of Adam and Eve has long been read as the story of two individual human beings, the first man and woman from whom all humanity descended. Modern genetics has shown that this literal interpretation cannot stand. There was no “first man” or “first woman” in the sense of a solitary breeding pair. Yet the truth of the narrative, properly understood illuminates the findings of the mineralistic sciences. We will re-appropriate these figures in order to help us understand them as the primal polarity of group spirit and group soul, and check the unending modulation of the one and many. 

The two figures are not physical individuals. They represent the group spirit/soul of nascent humanity. Adam (“the one taken from the earth”) stands for the unified “I-being” of humanity, or in other words, the collective spiritual identity from which all individual human spirits descend. Eve (“the animate one”, or “the mother of all living”) represents the group soul, the diversifying principle that gives form to the physical, etheric, and astral bodies of human beings across the Earth. Spiritually, we are *one*. All human souls share the same origin in the Adam-I. Physically, we are *many*. The Eve-soul differentiates itself into the varied forms and temperaments that allow souls to undergo the experience and training necessary for their evolution. 

The more esoteric connection between the names Yahweh and Eve goes even further. Yahweh, the “Earth Lord”, is the being who guides the development of the human “I” on Earth. The name Yahweh contains the Yodh, which represents the individual I-being, that glimmer of selfhood that descends into the human soul. Eve, as the “Earth Mother” embodies the accumulated forces of the Old Saturn, Old Sun, and Old Moon stages of Earth evolution, forces which build the physical, etheric, and astral bodies that serve as a dwelling place for the “I”. Thus in the name “Yahweh”, we have the union of the spiritual I (the Adam principle), with it’s concretized form (the Eve principle). This union is the true “first couple”, the foundational polarity of spirit and soul that makes human existence on Earth possible. With this in mind, the apparent contradiction between man’s genetic unity and physical diversity dissolves. The one is the Adam-I, the unified spiritual origin of all human beings. Every human soul, regardless of race or epoch, participates in the same I-being. This is partly why, despite all the outward differences, we can recognize one another as fellow humans, why the deepest spiritual development always leads to the experience of a universal brotherhood. The differences end up negligible. The “many” is the Eve-soul, the variable manifest as the variety of physical forms, temperaments, and cultural expressions. The Eve-soul is a collective soul that “gives birth” to the multiplicity of human forms. Hence, from a single spiritual stock, diversity in man can come about.

Through the development of the “I”, and reception of the Christ impulse, individuals can increasingly free themselves from the constraints of heredity and race. The Pauline saying, that “There is neither Jew nor Greek, there is neither slave nor free, there is neither male nor female; for you are all one in Christ Jesus” makes this goal express. The reunification of the many into one, *without* the loss of individuality, is a foregone conclusion.
