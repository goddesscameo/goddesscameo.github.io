Idle Talk in the Age of TikTok: On Linguistic Decay

Ts gt me crine fr 😭

9 minute read

Of all the human faculties, language is perhaps the most intimate and mysterious, being responsible for conjuring emotions in the soul, and drawing out latent potential, all by an invocation of symbols and signs that we use daily. In its original and true nature, language was Logos, the divine creative principle that ordered the cosmos. The Word was understood in the past as an active power, a vibration that participated directly in the essence of what it named. There existed no arbitrary divide between the signifier and the signified, sound and being been fused. To utter a true name was therefore not to label a thing from the outside, but to invoke its reality and align with its spiritual core. This ontological unity rendered language inherently sacramental. Blessings, curses, and ritual incantations were seen as having immediate metaphysical effects because speech was an act of cosmic participation, distinct from its conception today as mere formality or social convention. This sanctity gave rise to the concept of holy languages. Traditions revered Sanskrit, Hebrew, Examination Chinese, Quranic Arabic, Biblical Latin, and so on and so forth, as Marshall McLuhan rightfully commented, as revealed architectures, or novel vessels for truth. To think, pray, or compose in these languages was to operate within a (and the) divine framework, the only medium capable of housing certain transcendental realities. Furthermore, this unity was experienced sensibly. The resonance of a word was organically tied to its meaning. Phenomena like the Bouba/Kiki effect (where people intuitively associate rounded sounds with rounded shapes and vice versa) are thus interpreted as the surviving remnants of this primordial unity. They are fleeting proof that the human soul retains a memory of a time when form and essence, or sound and sense, were one.

An ancient view this is, if for the fact that it casts our contemporary experience of language into a puzzling relief. For, we do not live in a world of true names and efficacious words. We inhabit a universe of arbitrary signs, where cat could just as easily be “gato”, “neko”, or “Katze”, as a matter of social agreement and contingency (or in some cases a view which deprives language of spiritual significance but has to concede that it fits mechanically in one way or another) rather than an explicit connection with cosmic truth. There is not a discrepancy, this in fact evidences a great decay from language as a participatory event, into convention. The fall from logos is the story of language becoming unmoored from being, a process intrinsically linked to the evolution of consciousness itself. This shift is a necessary, if not arduous, stage in Spirit’s journey. It corresponds to the epochal awakening of the Consciousness Soul - the modern principle of self-reflective individualism. To achieve self-awareness and freedom, human consciousness had to turn decisively inward, withdrawing its projective participation from the world. This inward turn, mirroring modernity’s deterritorializing force, required the very severing of the ancient bond between word and thing. Language had to be drained of its innate albeit given meaning, so that the sovereign “I” could become capable of grasping meaning from within as well as without. The celebrated arbitrariness of the sign, therefore, is in a way a diagnostic mark of this age. What linguistics posits as a neutral principle is the very mechanism of alienation required for the birth of the modern ego. The word becomes an empty shell, a detached signifier, because the conscious self needed a neutral tool to wield, rather than a sacred power to which it was subject.

The historical indices of this fall are certainly tangible. The evolution from pictographic to phonetic writing is a tell-tale sign. Hieroglyphs and ideograms maintained an iconic, mimetic link to the world. For example, a picture of a sun still carried a trace of the sun’s essence. The alphabet, a triumph of abstraction, reduces speech to a code of neutral symbols representing pure sound. This form of language is the perfect technology (in the sense of techne) for the Consciousness Soul, seeing as it is analytic efficient, and severed from iconic presence. In addition to this, it enables the dissemination of abstract thought, but at the same time finalizes the divorce of the written word from the living cosmos. This abstraction was compounded by the “scientific” and nominalist worldviews, which exiled qualities and essences from the material world, leaving only quantities and names. Language, in this world, can only point to surfaces, and likewise the commonality which etymology sheds light on can only reasonably signify a correlation of concept with the convenience of spatiotemporal form. Furthermore, the Bouba/Kiki effect now appears in a tragic light as a testament to what was lost. It tells of old modes of consciousness and participatory magic (and language, back then, was by all means magic) flaring up in a mind otherwise conditioned to see sound and shape as separate domains. It is the soul’s muted protest against its own conceptual imprisonment.

Thus, the modern condition of language as a formal system, as a convenient but hollow geometry for the ego’s use, is the subjective reality of this objective historical stage. The feeling that language is an internal combinatory space is not its eternal truth, it is only what is left after the spirit of Logos has withdrawn. However, it is not all dire. The great emptying of meaning in the world, can also be considered a great preparation. It is the necessary negative moment in the process of totality. By passing through this desert of abstraction, by experiencing language as a detached and formal medium, the Consciousness Soul gains the unprecedented capacity for critical self-reflection and free conceptual recognition. It must lose the given, participatory Word, to eventually find it again, consciously and from within. The arbitrary language of the modern age is therefore the condition in which the possibility of a future redeemed speech is created. Having fully integrated the power of abstract thought, humanity will one day speak with the authority of Logos.

For now, we have to deal with what Heidegger called “Idle Talk”. The ultimate manifestation of this emptied, circulating language, is what Heidegger characterizes as speech that has completely lost its anchor in the primordial, disclosive power of Logos. It is language reduced to a self-referential social mechanism. Words are passed along not to reveal the truth of a matter, but precisely to avoid encountering it. In Idle Talk, understanding/substance (they are etymologically the same word, fun fact!) is simulated through the effortless absorption of pre-digested and “average” meanings. The reality of the arbitrary sign is a closed circuit of chatter where words refer only to other words, creating a comforting shallow consensus that shields the modern ego from the demanding world-disclosing responsibility of authentic speech. This is the sacred Word that once invoked being, having devolved, in its most common social form, into a background noise of cliché and hearsay- it is the sound of consciousness speaking to itself from within itself.

A descent into abstraction needs a habitat, and it finds this habitat in the digital realm of the internet, the perfect expression of modernity’s de-temporalized, deterritorialized space. Here, the inward-turning Consciousness Soul constructs a world in its own image featuring a realm of pure information where language exists in a state of frictionless circulation, liberated not only from the object, but from the being and body of the Earth. The internet is the ecosystem in which idle talk becomes the dominant, self-replicating mode of being, setting the state for a platform like TikTok to emerge as the apotheosis of post-literate communication.

The linguistic ecology of TikTok represents the logical result of this trajectory in the form of the metastasis of Idle Talk into an optimized and fully realized system. The platform’s hallmarks of gratuitous abbreviation, intentional misspellings, syntactic compression, and the rapid, vampiric repurposing of words (particularly from African American Vernacular English) into mimetic signals are the signature of language accelerating into pure social functionality, shedding any remaining pretensions to depth, history, or disclosure. When “because” becomes “bc”, or “this shit” becomes “ts”, or complex (in form, not in substance) statements are cramped into acronyms, we witness the final abstraction of the sign. The word lacks being even a full shell- it is fragment. It is a token whose value is determined solely by its velocity and adhesive power within the circuit of communal recognition.

This is Idle Talk weaponized and accelerated by the habitat of the internet. Heidegger’s concept feared a chatter that levels all understanding to the average, whereas TikTok’s architecture actively enforces it. Algorithmic virality rewards surface level novelty and in-group fluency, incentivizing the continuous production of linguistic shortcuts that bypass conscious reflection by the subject, in order to trigger instant affective or tribal response. The intentional “wrongness” of a spelling is a performative rejection of external authority, creating a closed vernacular that fortifies community boundaries on the one hand, and further divorces the sign from any stable referent on the other. “Rizz” and “sigma” are not in a dictionary (yet, as far as I know), but still exists in the collective performance of its use as a purely circulating social value.

In a sense, we can call this beyond illiteracy (although America has a major illiteracy problem, and you’d expect nothing less from a democratic nation), into post-literacy, defined as the linguistic state perfectly adapted to the Consciousness Soul’s internalization. Language has become a pure tool of the sovereign ego, constructing transient identities and affiliations in a digital das Man. However, even within this schema, even in the apparent decay, a purpose is served. TikTok language, as the extreme of Idle Talk, makes the alienation of the sign total and unmistakable. By reducing communication to its most fluid and disposable form, it forces a kind of crisis. One either surrenders to endless circulation for its own sake, or begins to feel the hunger for a Word that means. In this sense, the vernacular of the platform serves as a negative positivity. It is the resonant sound of the hollow Logos, waiting to be filed again by a spirit that has fully passed through the task of its own grasping, waiting to be recognized from within and from without.
